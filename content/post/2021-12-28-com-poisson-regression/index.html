Often times when I encounter a count distribution, the instinct is to fit a Poisson model. However, this makes a restrictive assumption of equidispersion (i.e., the population variance equals its mean). An alternative, in the common case of overdispersion (i.e., when the population variance is greater than its mean), is to fit a negative-binomial model. The less prominent case however, presumably due to its rarity in applications, is when the distribution is underdispersed (i.e., the population variance is less than its mean)â€“there seems to be a lower number of options to deal with this sort of data. This article explores a model suitable for this scenario, namely, the Conway-Maxwell Poisson model.
